Computer Ethics & Professional Responsibility

This course is dedicated to exploring the social, political, philosophy, constitutional, legal, and economic issues related to computers. In addition to the ethical issues related to computing, those that apply to all aspects of life – honesty, responsibility, fairness are discussed. The special issues that spring from these topics was discuessed-“Applied Ethics” and the common codes and professional practices of the CS and IT fields. A few examples of the topics discussed are below:

  • Ethical Theories (Relativism, Divine Command, Kantianism)
  • Ethics and the Internet (cyberbullying, cyberthreats, cybersurveillance, First Amendment rights, Federal Laws, and censorship)
  • Intellectual property (Trademarks, Patents, Copyrights, and nondisclosure contracts

Database Management Systems

This course is an introduction to the theory and practice of database management. It covers concepts for modeling organizational data and designing databases using state-of-the-art techniques. Topics include strategies and methodology for planning, designing and implementing databases with an emphasis on relational databases and some coverage of emerging NoSQL systems. Numerous real-world case examples are used to introduce the concepts. To reinforce the concepts and provide hands-on experience, state-of-art database management systems will be used extensively in the course. Ethical issues of concern to computer professionals will also be covered in this course, such as ethical and legal basis for privacy protection, privacy implications of massive database systems, technological strategies for privacy protection, freedom of expression in cyberspace, and international and intercultural implications. After successful completion of this course, I am able to:

  • Compare and contrast database types including relational, hierarchical, distributed, and other models
  • Describe the role of a database, a DBMS, and a database server within a complex system supporting multiple applications.
  • Design and deploy a simple database for a specified application.
